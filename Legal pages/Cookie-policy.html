Effective Date: November 22, 2024

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. Cookies allow the website to recognize your device, remember your preferences, and improve your browsing experience.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We use the following types of cookies on our website:

  • Essential Cookies: These cookies are necessary for the operation of our website, such as enabling secure login and remembering your privacy preferences.
  • Performance Cookies: These cookies help us analyze how visitors use our website, allowing us to improve its performance and functionality. These cookies do not collect personal data.
  • Functionality Cookies: These cookies allow us to remember your preferences (such as language settings) and personalize your experience on the site.
  • Targeting Cookies: These cookies are used to deliver advertisements that are relevant to you and your interests, and they help us track the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns.

3. How We Use Cookies

Cookies on Finxboxn are used for the following purposes:

  • To personalize your experience and tailor content based on your preferences.
  • To analyze site traffic and improve the performance of the website.
  • To serve relevant advertisements and track ad performance.
  • To maintain and improve site security.

4. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in the settings of your web browser. Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies, and you can choose whether to accept cookies or not. However, please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of some features on our website.

To learn more about how to control cookies, please refer to the help section of your browser:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox

  • Internet Explorer

For example, we may use Google Analytics to analyze traffic on our site. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect data, which helps us improve our website and understand how visitors interact with it. You can learn more about how Google uses data at Google's Privacy & Terms page.
